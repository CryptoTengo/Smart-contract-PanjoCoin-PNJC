⚠️ Risk Disclosure — PanjoCoin (PNJC)

---

## 1. Overview

PanjoCoin (PNJC) is a decentralized experimental digital asset deployed on the Polygon blockchain.

This document describes known and foreseeable risks associated with interacting with the PNJC protocol, including technical, financial, governance, and systemic blockchain risks.

By interacting with PNJC smart contracts, users acknowledge that they fully understand and accept these risks.

---

## 2. General Market Risk

Cryptocurrency markets are highly volatile and unpredictable.

Users acknowledge that:

- PNJC price may experience extreme volatility
- Token value may decrease significantly or become zero
- No guarantees of liquidity, demand, or price stability exist
- Market behavior is driven by external speculative forces beyond protocol control

---

## 3. Liquidity Risk (DEX-SPECIFIC RISK)

PNJC liquidity is provided through decentralized exchange pools (e.g., Uniswap / Quickswap).

Users acknowledge:

- Initial liquidity may be limited at launch
- High slippage may occur during early trading periods
- Price discovery is fully market-driven
- Liquidity depth may fluctuate over time

Although LP tokens are intended to be locked or burned, liquidity conditions remain subject to market dynamics.

---

## 4. Smart Contract Risk

PNJC operates through autonomous smart contracts deployed on-chain.

Users acknowledge:

- Smart contracts may contain unknown vulnerabilities or bugs
- Exploits may result in partial or total loss of funds
- Contract logic is immutable once deployed (unless explicitly designed otherwise)
- External audits reduce but do not eliminate risk

Mitigation measures include:

- Use of industry-standard libraries (e.g., OpenZeppelin)
- Modular contract architecture
- Separation of core protocol components

---

## 5. Governance Risk

PNJC governance is implemented via a hybrid model:

- Multisignature governance (current phase)
- DAO governance (future phase)

Users acknowledge:

- Governance rules may evolve over time
- Multisig participants retain operational control in early stages
- DAO governance is not fully decentralized at launch
- Parameter changes may occur via governance execution

---

## 6. Centralization Risk (EARLY STAGE DISCLOSURE)

During early protocol stages:

- Treasury operations are controlled via multisignature wallet
- Core protocol decisions may be executed by a limited set of signers
- DAO governance mechanisms are not yet fully active

This represents a transitional phase toward progressive decentralization.

---

## 7. Technical Risk

Blockchain infrastructure and external dependencies may introduce technical risks:

- Network congestion or downtime (Polygon or RPC providers)
- Increased gas fees during high demand periods
- Transaction delays or failures
- Wallet or DEX integration failures

---

## 8. Regulatory Risk

Cryptocurrency regulations vary across jurisdictions and may change over time.

Users acknowledge:

- PNJC may be subject to regulatory scrutiny
- Legal classification of tokens may differ by region
- Future regulatory changes may affect accessibility or usage
- Users are responsible for compliance with local laws

---

## 9. Oracle & External Dependency Risk (Future Scope)

If the protocol integrates external systems in future iterations:

- Oracle manipulation risks may exist
- External API failures may affect system behavior
- Dependency on third-party infrastructure may introduce systemic risk

---

## 10. Trust Model & System Boundaries

PNJC defines explicit separation of trust domains:

- Token Contract → immutable and non-mintable
- Liquidity Pools → locked or permanently burned after launch
- Treasury Wallet → controlled via multisignature governance
- DAO Layer → future decentralized governance system
- Team Allocation → managed via vesting contracts

No single entity has unilateral control over the entire system.
